Prohibited Items Policy

Items that may not be listed, sent, or carried through AfroRoute.

Effective: 8 April 2026 Platform: AfroRoute · afroroute.com Contact: support@afroroute.com

Purpose

This policy explains what may not be sent, carried, listed, requested, accepted, or transported through AfroRoute. Users are responsible for ensuring all items comply with airline rules, airport security rules, customs requirements, import/export laws, and applicable laws in all origin, transit, and destination countries.

If there is any doubt about whether an item is allowed — do not list it, send it, accept it, or carry it.

Illegal items

  • Illegal drugs or controlled substances
  • Stolen goods or counterfeit goods
  • Smuggled goods
  • Undeclared commercial goods where declaration is required
  • Items intended to evade customs, tax, or border controls
  • Items prohibited by the laws of origin, transit, or destination countries

Weapons and dangerous items

  • Firearms, ammunition, and explosives
  • Fireworks and grenades
  • Weapon parts and military-grade equipment
  • Stun guns, tasers, and electroshock weapons
  • Switchblades or knives prohibited by airline rules or applicable law
  • Weapon accessories prohibited by law or airline rules

Dangerous goods and hazardous materials

  • Flammable liquids, fuels, and gas canisters
  • Compressed gases and compressed air devices
  • Corrosive, toxic, or infectious substances
  • Radioactive materials and oxidisers
  • Poisons and chemicals
  • Hazardous batteries not allowed under airline passenger baggage rules
  • Any item classified as dangerous goods unless clearly permitted under applicable passenger baggage rules

Regulated or restricted goods

  • Prescription medicine unless legally permitted and properly documented
  • Narcotics or controlled medicines
  • Medical devices requiring special documentation
  • Alcohol and tobacco or nicotine products where restricted
  • Wildlife products, ivory, and endangered species products
  • Cultural artefacts
  • High-value goods requiring customs declaration unless properly declared
  • Cash or negotiable instruments above legal limits
  • Financial instruments intended for unlawful transfer

Perishables and biological items

  • Fresh food requiring temperature control
  • Meat, fish, dairy, plants, seeds, or agricultural products where import/export is restricted
  • Human remains
  • Biological samples, blood, or bodily fluids
  • Live animals

Sensitive or high-risk personal items

  • Passports, identity documents, visas, or official government documents belonging to another person
  • Bank cards or SIM cards intended for misuse
  • Passwords, access devices, authentication devices, or credentials
  • Private data storage devices where illegal or unauthorised transfer is suspected

Items that create unacceptable risk

  • Items with unclear ownership
  • Items that cannot be inspected
  • Sealed packages the Traveler cannot verify
  • Items inconsistent with the description or photos provided
  • Items that create reputational, legal, safety, or fraud risk

Traveler right to refuse

Travelers have the right and responsibility to refuse any item that is not documented, differs from the listing, appears unsafe or illegal, cannot be inspected, creates discomfort or suspicion, or violates this Policy.

A Traveler should never carry a package they cannot reasonably inspect and understand.

Sender declaration

By listing, handing over, or signing a shipment agreement, the Sender confirms that all items are accurately described, legally owned, legal to transport, and compliant with applicable airline, customs, and legal rules.

Customs and airline compliance

Approval by AfroRoute does not mean an item is approved by an airline, airport, customs authority, border authority, or law enforcement agency. Users are responsible for checking applicable rules before agreeing to send or carry any item.

Enforcement

AfroRoute may remove listings, block shipment requests, cancel agreements, suspend or terminate accounts, delay or block payouts, report activity to authorities, cooperate with investigations, and preserve records for legal or safety purposes.

Reporting prohibited items

If you believe a listing or package violates this Policy, contact us immediately at support@afroroute.com.
