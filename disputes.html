Dispute Resolution Policy

How AfroRoute handles disputes between Senders and Travelers.

Effective: 8 April 2026 Platform: AfroRoute · afroroute.com Contact: support@afroroute.com

Purpose

This policy explains how AfroRoute handles disputes between Senders, Travelers, and other Platform users. Our dispute process is designed to support trust, safety, and fair manual review. It does not replace legal claims, insurance claims, airline claims, customs proceedings, law enforcement reports, or court action.

Types of disputes

Disputes may include:

  • Package not delivered, damaged, or with missing items
  • Item mismatch or undisclosed contents
  • Delivery confirmation disagreement
  • Refusal at pickup or delivery
  • Payment or payout dispute
  • Suspected fraud or prohibited item concern
  • User misconduct or off-platform contact abuse
  • Identity or account concern

When to open a dispute

Open a dispute as soon as possible after discovering an issue. Recommended timeframes:

  • Pickup issue: within 24 hours of scheduled pickup
  • Delivery issue: within 24 hours of delivery or expected delivery
  • Payment or payout issue: within 7 days of payout-ready status
  • Prohibited item or safety issue: immediately

Late disputes may still be reviewed, but delay may limit available evidence and remedies.

Evidence we may review

AfroRoute may review the shipment agreement, item descriptions and photos, messages, pickup and delivery confirmations, delivery photos, recipient confirmation code records, timestamps, user identity verification status, payment records, support communications, and user history.

Users must cooperate and provide truthful information.

Payout hold during disputes

If a dispute is opened before payout release, AfroRoute may place the payout on hold until review is completed. AfroRoute may refuse to mark a shipment as payout-ready or may reverse payout-ready status if a dispute, fraud concern, safety concern, or policy violation exists.

Review process

  1. Receive dispute report
  2. Notify involved parties
  3. Collect evidence
  4. Review agreement and delivery records
  5. Request additional information if needed
  6. Decide outcome

AfroRoute aims to review disputes within a reasonable time. Complex disputes may take longer.

Possible outcomes

Depending on the evidence, AfroRoute may release payout to Traveler, delay or deny payout, refund Sender where appropriate, request additional documentation, suspend or terminate accounts, block future transactions, preserve records, or refer users to authorities or legal processes.

User responsibilities

  • Keep communication on AfroRoute where possible
  • Document pickup and delivery accurately
  • Sign agreements truthfully
  • Not submit false or abusive claims
  • Respond to AfroRoute requests promptly
  • Preserve relevant evidence
  • Comply with all Platform policies
False or abusive disputes may lead to account restriction or termination.

Prohibited items and illegal activity

If a dispute involves suspected prohibited, dangerous, restricted, illegal, stolen, counterfeit, or undeclared goods, AfroRoute may suspend review, restrict accounts, preserve evidence, and cooperate with authorities.

No guarantee

AfroRoute does not guarantee recovery of lost items, payment recovery, refund approval, payout approval, or a specific dispute outcome.

Contact

Submit a dispute through the Platform or email disputes@afroroute.com.
